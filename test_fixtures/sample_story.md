# The Lighthouse Keeper's Tea

<!-- @subtitle -->

<!-- @body -->

The lamp had not lit on time, which was the first thing Rowan noticed
when she came up from the kitchen with the kettle still in her hand.
She set it down on the iron grating without thinking and watched the
beam stutter — once, twice, three times — before it caught.

She drank her tea standing up, the way she always did when something
was wrong.

---

Outside, the storm had got the rocks talking. Somewhere below the
catwalk a wave hit hard enough that she felt it through her boots.
The lamp held steady now, throwing its long stripe out across the
black, and she counted the rotations the way her father had taught
her when she was small. Twelve seconds. Twelve seconds. Twelve.

By dawn the wind had dropped and she still had not finished the tea.
