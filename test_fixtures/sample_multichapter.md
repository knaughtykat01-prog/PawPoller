# Chapter 1: First Tide

**⟨ Mira ⟩**

The harbour was not yet awake. She walked the length of the breakwater
twice before the gulls noticed her, then stopped at the far end and
sat down on the wet stone.

> The first thing her grandmother had taught her about the sea was
> that the sea did not, under any circumstance, owe her anything.

Out past the bell-buoy the water was lifting in a slow rolling way
that she had never quite learned to read. She tried again, and gave
up, and watched it instead.

# Chapter 2: Second Tide

**⟨ Hatch ⟩**

By the time he reached the lighthouse the rain had come on properly
and the path was running with it. He kicked the wet sand off his
boots at the door, then thought better of it and kicked them off
entirely, and walked the last flight in his socks.

# Chapter 3: Third Tide

**⟨ Mira ⟩**

The storm broke at four in the morning, the way storms in this part
of the world preferred to break — politely, almost apologetically,
without any of the drama you might expect from the noise it had been
making all night.

Mira watched the sky come up grey, then pewter, then a colour she
had no honest word for, and then drank the last of the cold tea
because it was there.

It was, she decided, going to be a long day. They were all going to
be long days now. She did not, on balance, mind.
