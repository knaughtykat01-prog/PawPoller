Mariner's Compass

A short test story for PawPoller QA

By Test Author

I. Departure

The harbour was quiet at four in the morning, which suited Hatch well enough. He liked the work that nobody else wanted — the hour before the gulls, the first slap of the rope on a wet deck.

"You'll lose the tide if you don't move," his uncle had told him once, and the line had stuck.

He moved.

II. The crossing

Three things became true on the second night out:

  • The compass needle had begun to drift west.
  • The radio had stopped pretending to receive anything.
  • He was, by any honest accounting, lost.

He kept the bow into the swell and waited for morning.
