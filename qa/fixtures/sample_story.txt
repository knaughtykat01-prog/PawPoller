The Late Train

A short test story for PawPoller QA.

By Test Author.


There was a particular kind of disappointment to a train that was
nearly on time. Sixteen minutes late was an event. Two minutes late
was an insult. The 7:14 had pulled into platform three at 7:16, and
Mira had spent both of those minutes watching the indicator board
update itself in increments she could no longer trust.

She got on anyway. There was nothing else to do.

The carriage was almost empty. A man at the far end was eating cold
chips from a paper bag with the studied calm of someone who had
decided that the day was already over. A woman in the seat behind him
was reading a paperback so battered that it had moved past being a
book and into being an object of furniture.

Mira sat down by the window. The doors closed. The train, having
nothing to apologise for, did not.


End of The Late Train.
